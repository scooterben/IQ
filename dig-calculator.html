DIG Score Calculator

Four-factor scoring model for memorabilia appraisal. Score is 0–100 and combines provenance specificity, artist tier, object class, and curatorial importance. Internal use only — do not share formula weights.

P
Provenance Specificity
40% weight
—
How specifically can the item be placed at a culturally defining moment? The more precise and the more culturally resonant the moment, the higher the score.
A
Artist Tier
25% weight
—
Where does the artist sit in the cultural canon? Tier 1 artists are those who fundamentally shaped an era — universally canonical across generations.
O
Object Class
20% weight
—
What type of object is it? Instruments that were actually played score highest. Unique or custom-made objects command the top tier.
H
Curatorial Importance
15% weight
—
Expert curatorial rating: how important is this item within its category, relative to all items by this artist and in this genre? 10 = most important example in existence.
5
1 — Minor significance 5 — Notable 10 — Most important

Score Formula
DIG = (P/4 × 40) + (A/4 × 25)
        + (O/4 × 20) + (H/10 × 15)

Range: 0 – 100
