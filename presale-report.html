Methodology

How DIG Reserve Recommendations Are Calculated

The DIG Score
Each lot receives a DIG Score (0–100) based on Dig Appraisals' proprietary four-factor model: Provenance Specificity, Artist Tier, Object Class, and Curatorial Importance. The score reflects the intrinsic quality of the item independent of current market conditions.
Score Tiers & Implied Reserve Logic
85–100
Reserve = mid-estimate
70–84
Reserve = low est. × 0.90
55–69
Reserve = low est. × 0.70
<55
Reserve = low est. × 0.50
Comparables & Confidence
Where recent comparable sales exist (within 36 months, same artist, similar object class), recommendations are anchored to those results and adjusted for market conditions. Confidence level (●●●) reflects the depth of comparable data. Three dots = 3+ direct comparables. One dot = estimate derived primarily from DIG Score tier alone.

Back-Test Performance
Across 35 real consignment lots scored by Dig Appraisals over the period 2002–2026, DIG Score–implied reserves were validated against actual realised prices. Museum Grade lots (85+) achieved an average of 3.7× the DIG-recommended reserve. No Museum Grade lot bought in when the DIG reserve recommendation was followed.
